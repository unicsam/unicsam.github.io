Your phone is the remote.

Turn your Android device into a wireless keyboard, mouse, touchpad, gamepad, and TV remote. Connects directly over Bluetooth — no Wi-Fi required, no companion software on the target device.

What it does

Six input modes. One app.

Keyboard, mouse, touchpad, TV remote, air mouse, and gamepad — all in a single app, switching between modes instantly.

Keyboard & Numpad

Type passwords, search queries, URLs, and messages directly from your phone. Full numpad for spreadsheets and data entry.

Touchpad & Mouse

Cursor control with left and right click, smooth scrolling, and precise navigation — works like a laptop trackpad.

TV Remote

D-pad navigation, home and back buttons, media controls, and volume adjustment for Android TV and Google TV.

Air Mouse

Control the cursor using your phone's motion sensors. Tilt and move your phone to navigate on supported devices.

Gamepad

Use your phone as a Bluetooth game controller for compatible devices and media centers.

Multi-device switching

Pair and save multiple devices. Switch between them instantly with one tap. Auto-reconnects to your last device.

How it works

No Wi-Fi. No server software. No setup on the target.

OneLink uses standard Bluetooth HID protocols. Your phone appears as a real wireless keyboard and mouse to the connected device.

1

Pair over Bluetooth

Open OneLink Remote and start advertising. On the target device, go to Bluetooth settings and pair with your phone.

2

Connect

Once paired, the target device recognizes your phone as a standard Bluetooth keyboard and mouse. No apps or drivers needed on the target.

3

Control

Switch between keyboard, touchpad, TV remote, air mouse, and gamepad modes depending on what you're controlling.

Compatibility

Any device that supports standard Bluetooth keyboards and mice will work with OneLink. No special software required on the target.

Connection Setup
Windows pairing
  1. Enable Bluetooth on your Windows PC.
  2. Open OneLink Remote and tap "Advertise / Start Pairing".
  3. On Windows, go to Settings → Bluetooth & Devices → Add Device → Bluetooth.
  4. Select your phone from the list. Windows will recognize it as a standard keyboard and mouse.
macOS pairing
  1. Open System Settings → Bluetooth on your Mac.
  2. Start pairing mode in OneLink Remote.
  3. Click Connect next to your phone in the Bluetooth panel.
  4. Grant keyboard simulation approval if prompted by macOS security.
Android TV / Smart TV pairing
  1. On your TV, go to Settings → Remotes & Accessories → Add Accessory.
  2. Start pairing in OneLink Remote.
  3. Select the controller from the list on your TV. You can now use the virtual D-pad and touchpad to navigate.
